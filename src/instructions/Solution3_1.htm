Solution 3.1 - Race conditions in the producer-consumer problem.

Answers to Questions

You were asked to experiment with different speeds for the producer and the consumer, and depending on these speeds you are likely to have observed the following:

  1. When the consumer starts before the producer (or when the consumer is faster than the producer) a NullPointerException may occur in the Consumer. This is an indication that the consumer has tried to read from a location, before any data has been placed there.

  2. When the producer is faster than the consumer: the producer has input numbers 10 to 29, but the consumer may have only consumed numbers 20 to 29, for example. This is an indication that the producer overwrites the data before it has had a chance of being consumed: an example of the lost update problem.

    It is also possible that the consumer consumes in an odd order, say from 10, to 19, to 16. In that case there are problems in reading the values of the shared variables in, out and count.
